Purpose of Project 
TechSpace is growing, and the leadership question is simple: are we scaling profitably, and where do we need to intervene before risk shows up in the P&L?  This portfolio review consolidates financial results, customer value, category mix, store performance, and operations into a single decision view for the Executive Leadership Team.

Key Findings
1) Profit is strong, but the portfolio is concentrated
TechSpace delivers a 55.93% gross margin and $112.3M in gross profit, with 35.98% generated online. However, the Top 3 categories account for 67% of revenue, led by Computers and Home Appliances. Profit stability is increasingly dependent on a narrow revenue base.

2) Revenue quality is supported by repeat customers
Customer economics are solid: 62.71% repeat (L12M), $4,693 ARPC, and 13,563 active customers. Retention holds across cohorts, and ARPC varies materially by market, identifying higher-value regions. Growth is supported by customer quality rather than discount-driven acquisition.

3) Some high-volume categories may be diluting margin
Several high-volume categories contribute disproportionately less to profit, concentrating margin strength in fewer segments. Higher-value customers skew toward stronger-margin categories, and demand composition shifts as stores mature. Mix discipline will determine margin durability.

4) Store exposure is measurable and actionable
The portfolio includes 66 active stores, with 17 at-risk stores representing 20.93% of six-month revenue. Gaps reflect either under-conversion in strong-demand markets or stagnation in declining regions. The exposure is defined and actionable.

5) Operations is a strength, but must scale ahead of demand
Operational performance remains strong: 3.4-day average delivery time alongside 38.52% online share, with service improving as volume grows. Capacity must scale ahead of demand to preserve this advantage to protect service levels 

Strategic Priorities
•	Protect the profit engine
Maintain margin discipline in Computers and Home Appliances; treat these as “non-negotiable” profit anchors while online grows.
•	Reduce concentration risk deliberately
Lower reliance on the Top 3 categories by improving margin mix in high-volume SKUs and growing adjacent categories associated with high-value customers.
•	Act on store exposure now
Prioritize the 17 at-risk stores with a clear plan: fix efficiency where demand exists, and consider exit/downsizing where demand is structurally weak.
•	Scale operations ahead of demand inflection
Use online growth trajectory and order density to guide capacity planning so delivery performance remains stable as online share increases.
